Project: TeamFlow – Lightweight Project Management with Task Tracking and Team Chat

Description: Small teams — freelance groups, startup squads, student project groups —
do not need the full complexity of enterprise tools like Jira or the distraction of having
to switch between a task manager and a separate messaging app. We want a single, simple
tool where a team of 3 to 15 people can create tasks, assign them, set deadlines, track
progress, and chat in the context of the project — all in one place. It should be something
a team can set up in 10 minutes and actually use every day without training. As someone
who has coordinated multiple small project teams, the tools that get used are the simple
ones, not the powerful ones nobody understands.

General Needs & Background
Small teams often start with good intentions — a shared spreadsheet, a Trello board, a
WhatsApp group — but these quickly fall apart when tasks are spread across different tools,
updates get buried in chat, and nobody knows what the current priority is. A unified tool
that keeps tasks and team communication together means less context-switching and a
clearer shared understanding of progress. The key insight is that small teams do not need
advanced features like Gantt charts, resource allocation, or time tracking — they need to
answer three questions clearly: What needs to be done? Who is doing it? Is it done yet?
Everything else is secondary.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Small team members (designers, developers, coordinators) working on shared
projects, team leads or project managers in SMEs and startups, student project groups
Secondary users: Clients or external partners with read-only access to project progress,
freelancers managing multiple concurrent client projects, team leads at NGOs or community
organisations
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, small business and startup community
advisors
Indirect stakeholders: HR and operations managers in growing companies, project
management professional bodies, productivity software market analysts

What We Want the Tool to Do
- Allow teams to create a project with a name, description, and list of members.
- Let anyone on the team create a task: title, description, assignee, due date, priority level.
- Support simple status columns: To Do, In Progress, In Review, Done (like a basic Kanban).
- Allow comments on individual tasks so discussion stays attached to the relevant work.
- Include a team chat channel per project for general conversation without leaving the tool.
- Send notifications when a task is assigned, moved, or commented on.
- Show a project overview: tasks due this week, overdue tasks, and who is overloaded.
- Allow file attachment on tasks (design files, documents, links).
- Let a team lead set a project deadline and see overall progress as a simple percentage.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Setup should take under 10 minutes — no lengthy onboarding or admin configuration.
- The tool must be free for small teams (up to 10 members) — most small teams cannot pay.
- Must work reliably in the browser — no desktop installation required.
- Mobile access is important — team members check tasks on their phone throughout the day.
- Must not overwhelm users with features — every element on screen should have a clear purpose.

What Good Looks Like
- The whole team knows the current status of the project without a status meeting.
- Tasks do not fall through the cracks because they are assigned and tracked in one place.
- Team chat is focused on work, not scattered across WhatsApp and email.
- A new team member can understand the full project state within five minutes of joining.
