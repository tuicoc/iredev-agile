Project: MoveIt – Platform Connecting People Who Need Help Moving Items with Local Drivers

Description: Moving a sofa, a fridge, or a pile of boxes across town sounds simple,
but finding a trustworthy person with a van or truck to help — at short notice, at a fair
price — is surprisingly hard. Hiring a full moving company is overkill and expensive for
small moves. Asking friends is unreliable. There is no easy platform in most cities where
someone can post "I need to move a washing machine tomorrow, who can help?" and get
matched with a local driver with the right vehicle. We want a platform that works like
a ride-hailing app but for moving small to medium loads, with transparent pricing, basic
safety features, and ratings on both sides.

General Needs & Background
There is a large informal market of drivers who use their personal van or pick-up truck
to earn extra income by helping people move items. Right now, these drivers get work
through word of mouth or unorganised Facebook posts. On the customer side, people
post in local community groups and hope someone responds in time. Both sides would
benefit from a structured platform that matches supply to demand, handles basic
communication, shows pricing upfront, and builds trust through reviews. This is not
about large commercial removals — it is about the everyday person who bought a second-
hand wardrobe and needs help getting it home.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: People needing to move items locally (buyers of second-hand furniture,
people relocating, businesses moving office supplies), local drivers with vans or trucks
seeking flexible earning opportunities
Secondary users: Local courier and logistics businesses offering overflow capacity,
apartment building managers coordinating move-in/move-out schedules
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, logistics and transport advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Local transport regulators, insurance providers, second-hand
marketplaces (e.g., Facebook Marketplace, Carousell) as referral sources

What We Want the Platform to Do
- Let customers post a move request: pick-up location, drop-off location, item description,
  size/weight estimate, preferred time, and maximum budget.
- Allow drivers to browse nearby requests and accept jobs that suit their vehicle and schedule.
- Show customers nearby available drivers with vehicle type, ratings, and estimated price.
- Calculate a suggested price based on distance and item size as a starting point.
- Enable basic in-app chat between customer and driver once a job is accepted.
- Allow both sides to rate and review each other after the job.
- Send notifications to the customer when the driver is on the way and when they arrive.
- Show job history for drivers to track their earnings.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Safety is critical — there must be basic identity verification for drivers.
- Payment should be handled in-app or via a trusted method — no cash disputes.
- Must work in areas with mixed internet connectivity.
- Pricing must be transparent before commitment — no surprise charges.
- The platform should not require drivers to be registered businesses — freelancers welcome.

What Good Looks Like
- A customer can find a driver for a small move within a few hours of posting.
- Drivers build a reputation through reviews and get steady local bookings.
- The move happens at a fair, agreed price with no awkward cash negotiation.
- Both sides feel safe and informed throughout the process.
