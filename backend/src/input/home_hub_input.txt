Project: HomeHub – Unified Dashboard to Monitor and Control Smart Home Devices

Description: Smart home devices are becoming more common, but the biggest frustration
for homeowners is that every brand has its own separate app. I have lights from one
brand, a security camera from another, a smart air conditioner, and a smart door lock —
and I need to open four different apps to control them. It is confusing, especially for
older family members at home. We want a single dashboard — on a phone or tablet or
browser — where we can see and control all our devices in one place, set simple
automations like "turn off all lights at midnight," and check if everything is on or off
before we leave the house.

General Needs & Background
The smart home market is growing, but integration between brands is a major pain point
for everyday users. Non-technical homeowners buy devices based on price or features,
not compatibility, and end up with an unmanageable collection of apps. A unified
interface that speaks to common smart home protocols (like Wi-Fi, Zigbee, or cloud APIs)
would make the smart home experience accessible to the average family — not just
tech enthusiasts. The dashboard should be simple enough for a 60-year-old parent to
understand but powerful enough for the person who set everything up to automate
daily routines.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Homeowners with multiple smart devices, family members sharing a smart
home (including older adults and children)
Secondary users: Property managers overseeing multiple units, home security monitoring
staff, home automation enthusiasts
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, home technology consultants
Indirect stakeholders: Smart device manufacturers (e.g., Philips Hue, TP-Link, Samsung),
internet service providers, energy utility companies

What We Want the Dashboard to Do
- Show all connected smart devices in one screen, grouped by room (e.g., living room,
  bedroom, kitchen).
- Allow basic controls: on/off toggle, brightness slider for lights, temperature setting for
  AC, lock/unlock for doors.
- Support automation rules: e.g., "If it is after 10 PM, dim living room lights to 30%."
- Show energy usage data where the device supports it.
- Send an alert if a device behaves unexpectedly (e.g., door unlocked when nobody is home).
- Allow different access levels: owner (full control), family member (limited control),
  guest (view only).
- Work on both mobile and a wall-mounted tablet.
- Support at least the most popular brands and work with third-party integrations.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Non-technical family members must be able to use it without reading a manual.
- Must be secure — no unauthorised access to home devices.
- Should not require replacing existing devices — it must work with what people already own.
- Must remain functional even if one device brand's cloud service is down (partial control).
- Response time for controls should feel instant — delays are frustrating and erode trust.

What Good Looks Like
- The whole family uses one app instead of four.
- Leaving the house means checking one screen and tapping "Away Mode."
- Automations run reliably without constant manual adjustments.
- Older family members are confident controlling lights and the aircon without help.
