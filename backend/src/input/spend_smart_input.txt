Project: SpendSmart – Personal Expense Tracking App with Automatic Spending Categories

Description: Most people have a rough idea that they are spending too much, but no clear
picture of where the money actually goes. Writing expenses in a notebook is tedious and
people stop doing it after a week. Bank statements are hard to read and do not give
a useful breakdown by category. We want a simple app where a user can quickly log what
they spent — or connect their bank account — and the app automatically sorts expenses
into categories like food, transport, entertainment, and rent, then shows a clear monthly
summary. As a personal finance advisor, I regularly see clients who genuinely do not
know how much they spend on coffee or takeaway. Awareness alone changes behaviour.

General Needs & Background
Personal budgeting tools either do too much (complex investment features, bank integrations
that require sensitive login sharing) or too little (just a list of transactions with no
insight). What most people need is something between a spreadsheet and a banking app —
a friendly, visual tracker that helps them see patterns without overwhelming them. Manual
entry should be fast (under 10 seconds per transaction), and automatic categorisation
should be accurate enough that users rarely need to correct it. A monthly budget-setting
feature and a simple alert when a category is approaching its limit would help people
adjust before it is too late.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Individuals managing personal finances, young adults and students budgeting
on limited income, couples tracking household expenses
Secondary users: Personal finance coaches and advisors using the data summaries with
clients, accountants reviewing spending patterns for self-employed clients
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, financial literacy programme advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Banks and financial institutions, consumer protection agencies,
government financial literacy initiatives

What We Want the App to Do
- Allow users to log an expense in under 10 seconds: amount, category, optional note.
- Auto-suggest a category based on the description (e.g., "McDonald's" → Food, "Grab" →
  Transport).
- Let users set a monthly budget for each category and track progress against it.
- Show a clear monthly summary: total spent, spent by category, vs. last month.
- Send a notification when a category reaches 80% of its budget.
- Allow splitting a single expense across multiple categories (e.g., a supermarket trip
  with both groceries and household items).
- Support recurring expenses (rent, subscriptions) so they are automatically included each month.
- Export a summary as a spreadsheet or PDF for tax or review purposes.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Many users are not comfortable linking their real bank account — manual entry must be
  a fully supported option.
- The app must be usable without a financial background — no jargon.
- Data must be private and not used for advertising.
- Must support multiple currencies for users who travel or earn in different currencies.
- Must work offline — people log expenses at the moment of purchase, often without Wi-Fi.

What Good Looks Like
- Users know exactly where their money goes without spending hours on a spreadsheet.
- Monthly overspending is caught early, not discovered at end of month.
- After three months, a user can see clear trends and make intentional changes.
- The categorisation is accurate enough that users trust it without checking every entry.
