Project: GreenTrace – Personal Carbon Footprint Tracking App for Daily Activities

Description: People generally want to live more sustainably, but they do not know where
to start because they cannot see the impact of their daily choices. Flying once versus
taking the train, eating meat every day versus a few times a week, driving versus cycling
— these choices have very different carbon costs, but nobody carries that knowledge in
their head. As a sustainability educator, I see a lot of guilt but not a lot of informed
action. We want a simple app that helps everyday people log their daily activities —
transport, food, energy use, purchases — and see their estimated carbon footprint in
a way that is meaningful and motivating, not guilt-inducing. The goal is awareness and
gradual improvement, not perfection.

General Needs & Background
Carbon footprint calculators already exist, but most are one-time tools — you fill in
a survey, get a number, and forget about it. A daily tracking app would create habits
around awareness, the same way calorie-counting apps changed how people think about food.
The key challenge is making data entry easy enough that users actually do it — complex
emission factor tables and scientific jargon will drive people away. The app should use
simple categories (how did you get to work today? what did you eat for lunch?), provide
rough but honest estimates, and show progress over weeks and months. A community or
challenge feature would help users feel part of a broader movement rather than alone.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Environmentally conscious individuals wanting to understand and reduce
their personal carbon footprint, students learning about sustainability
Secondary users: Sustainability educators and programme coordinators, corporate HR teams
running employee green challenges, community environmental groups
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, environmental science advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Government environmental agencies, carbon offset providers,
sustainable product and service companies, climate NGOs

What We Want the App to Do
- Let users log daily activities across key categories: transport (car, bus, flight, cycling),
  food (meat, vegetarian, vegan, food waste), home energy (electricity, heating), and
  shopping (new clothes, electronics, etc.).
- Estimate the carbon cost of each activity in kilograms of CO₂ using standard, cited
  reference values.
- Show a daily, weekly, and monthly carbon total with a comparison to the average person
  in their country.
- Highlight the one or two biggest contributors to the user's footprint each week.
- Suggest one small, practical action each week to reduce footprint (e.g., swap one meal).
- Allow users to join or create a group challenge (e.g., "our office tries to reduce
  footprint by 10% this month").
- Show progress over time as a positive trend — rewarding reduction, not punishing high days.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Emission estimates do not need to be scientifically exact — they need to be honest,
  consistent, and cited so users can trust them.
- The daily log should take under two minutes to complete.
- The tone must be encouraging, not guilt-driven or preachy.
- Must be free to use — sustainability tools should not be behind a paywall.
- Should work offline and sync when connected — users log at the moment of activity.

What Good Looks Like
- After one month, users clearly understand which habits drive most of their footprint.
- Users make at least one lasting behaviour change they can directly attribute to the app.
- Group challenges create social motivation in workplaces and community groups.
- The app feels empowering, not overwhelming or judgmental.
