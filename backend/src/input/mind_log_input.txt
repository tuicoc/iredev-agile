Project: MindLog – Personal Mood, Stress, and Daily Habit Tracking App

Description: Many people go through periods of stress, low motivation, or emotional
difficulty without realising it until things have already gotten worse. Tracking how you
feel each day — even in small ways — can help people notice patterns, identify what
drains or restores their energy, and make better decisions about their lifestyle. As a
counsellor working with young adults, I see many clients who cannot describe when their
symptoms started or what seems to make them better or worse. A simple daily check-in
app that tracks mood, sleep, stress level, and basic habits would give both users and
their support network a clearer picture over time, without requiring clinical knowledge
to use.

General Needs & Background
Many people want to take care of their mental wellbeing but feel overwhelmed by complex
apps or clinical-looking tools. A simple, private, and encouraging daily log can build
a habit of self-awareness. Users should be able to record how they feel, what they did,
and how they slept in less than two minutes. Over time, the app should show patterns —
like "you tend to feel worse on Sundays" or "your mood improves on days you exercise."
This is not a medical app and should never diagnose anything — it is purely a personal
reflection and awareness tool. It should feel warm, not clinical.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Individuals managing daily stress, students, young professionals seeking
self-awareness tools
Secondary users: Mental health counsellors and therapists (whose clients may share
summaries), wellness coaches, supportive friends or family members with shared access
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, wellbeing centre advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Mental health policymakers, employee wellness programme
coordinators, healthcare professionals

What We Want the App to Do
- Offer a short daily check-in: mood rating (1–5 or emoji-based), stress level, sleep hours,
  and a free-text note.
- Allow users to track habits they define themselves (e.g., exercise, water intake, screen
  time, journaling).
- Show a weekly and monthly summary with simple charts showing mood and habit trends.
- Highlight correlations in plain language (e.g., "On days you slept more than 7 hours,
  your mood averaged higher").
- Send a gentle daily reminder at a time the user chooses.
- Keep all data private and stored only on the user's device or under their account.
- Allow users to export a summary as a PDF to share with a counsellor if they choose.
- Never show alarming language or suggest diagnoses — keep tone supportive and neutral.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- The daily check-in must take under two minutes so users actually stick with it.
- Data belongs to the user — no selling or sharing without explicit consent.
- The app must be usable without an internet connection for offline journalling.
- Must be accessible to people with low digital literacy — no complex menus.
- Should be gentle in design — calming colours, no harsh notifications.

What Good Looks Like
- Users build a consistent habit of checking in without feeling pressured.
- After a month, they can clearly see which habits correlate with better or worse days.
- Counsellors report that clients come to sessions with better self-awareness.
- The app never makes users feel judged, diagnosed, or alarmed.
