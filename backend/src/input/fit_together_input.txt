Project: FitTogether – Workout Planning and Progress Sharing App for Friends

Description: Working out alone is hard to sustain. Many people start a fitness routine
with good intentions but drop off within a few weeks because there is nobody to stay
accountable to. Fitness coaches and personal trainers already know that having a training
partner or group dramatically improves consistency. We want an app where friends can
create and share workout routines with each other, log their progress, cheer each other
on, and even challenge each other to small friendly goals — without needing to buy an
expensive gym membership app or share a gym account. It should feel social and
motivating, not like a spreadsheet.

General Needs & Background
Most workout apps are designed for solo use — they track your runs or count your reps
but do not connect you with the people you actually work out with. Friends who train
together want to see each other's progress, share routines that worked for them, and
celebrate milestones together. Group fitness instructors want a way to send a weekly plan
to their informal group without having to message everyone individually. The social
element — seeing a friend complete their workout — is often more motivating than any
notification from an app. The app should make group fitness feel connected even when
people are working out at different times or in different places.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Fitness enthusiasts who exercise in friend groups or informal gym buddies,
individuals seeking workout accountability partners
Secondary users: Personal trainers and group fitness instructors, sports coaches managing
amateur teams, gym community managers
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, sports and recreation advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Gym facilities and equipment brands, sports medicine professionals,
health insurance wellness programmes

What We Want the App to Do
- Let users create workout routines (exercises, sets, reps, rest times) and share them
  directly with friends.
- Show a friend feed where you can see who completed a workout today and leave a
  reaction or comment.
- Allow users to log each session: exercises done, weight used, how they felt.
- Track progress over time with simple charts (e.g., total workouts per week, personal bests).
- Support friendly challenges — e.g., "Who does the most workouts this month?"
- Let a trainer or group leader create a weekly plan and push it to all group members.
- Send a motivational nudge when a friend logs a workout but you have not yet today.
- Allow workouts to be built from a library of common exercises with descriptions and images.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- The app should work without a gym or equipment — bodyweight routines must be supported.
- Friends must be able to form a private group — no public sharing without consent.
- Must be usable offline so people can log workouts at gyms with poor wifi.
- Should not require fitness expertise to create a routine — beginner-friendly.
- No subscription required for core features — social and logging functions should be free.

What Good Looks Like
- Friends consistently work out more often because they can see each other's activity.
- A trainer can manage a group of 10–15 people without using WhatsApp for workout plans.
- Users stick with a routine for longer because of the social accountability.
- Progress over months is visible and motivating.
