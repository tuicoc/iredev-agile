Project: GameDay – Platform for Organising and Joining Local Gaming Events and Tournaments

Description: The local gaming community — whether it is tabletop, card games, esports,
or console tournaments at a LAN café — is active and growing, but it has no dedicated
home online. Event organisers currently use Facebook Events, WhatsApp groups, or Discord
servers that are hard to find if you are new to the community. Players who want to join
a local tournament have no single place to look. We want a platform where anyone can
create a local gaming event, set the format, take registrations, manage brackets, and
communicate with participants — and where players can discover and join events in their
city without needing to know the organiser personally.

General Needs & Background
Grassroots gaming communities are built on personal connections and word of mouth,
which limits how much they can grow. New players want to find events but do not know
where to look. Organisers want more participants but their reach is limited to existing
followers. A dedicated platform, even a simple one, would help both sides. Tournament
organisers need basic bracket management so they do not have to use spreadsheets during
an event. Players want to see upcoming events near them, register with a few taps, and
know what to bring and when to show up. Venue owners (LAN cafés, board game cafés)
want to attract events to fill their space on slow days.

Relevant Stakeholders
Primary users: Gaming event organisers (individuals or gaming clubs), players looking for
local tournaments or casual gaming sessions to join
Secondary users: LAN café and board game café owners hosting events, gaming community
managers, game publishers running regional promotions
Project stakeholders: Project team, course lecturer, gaming community advisors
Indirect stakeholders: Game retailers and distributors, esports organisations and sponsors,
local youth recreation departments

What We Want the Platform to Do
- Let organisers create an event with: game title, format (tournament, casual, team-based),
  date, time, location, max participants, entry fee (if any), and rules.
- Allow players to discover events by city, game type, or date.
- Support online registration and a waiting list if the event is full.
- Provide a basic bracket generator for single elimination and round-robin formats.
- Allow the organiser to post updates and announcements to all registered participants.
- Let players build a simple profile showing their game preferences and event history.
- Allow ratings and comments after an event so the community can identify good organisers.
- Support both free and paid events, with the organiser managing payments externally.

Practical Limits We Are Working With
- Organisers range from experienced tournament directors to first-time community members
  — the interface must be simple for both.
- No in-app payment processing required at first — the focus is on discovery and registration.
- Events can be indoors (LAN cafés, community halls) or online — both should be supported.
- The platform should be free for community organisers — monetisation should not block access.
- Must work well on mobile since most players check events on their phones.

What Good Looks Like
- A new player in a city can find a local gaming event within five minutes.
- Organisers can run a 32-person tournament with brackets without using a spreadsheet.
- Venues see an increase in bookings from community events.
- The local gaming community grows because newcomers can actually find it.
